Version: 1.0

# Purpose

This glossary establishes the canonical vocabulary of Mimo.

Its purpose is to ensure consistency across:

- product strategy
- UX design
- engineering
- documentation
- AI-assisted design tools
- AI-assisted development tools

All contributors should prefer glossary terms over inventing alternative terminology.

When multiple terms could describe the same concept, the glossary definition takes precedence.

---

# Capture

## Definition

The act of preserving discovered information for future use.

Capture is the moment information enters Mimo.

---

## Example

Saving an Instagram reel through the Android share sheet.

---

## Why It Matters

Capture is the first step in the value chain.

Without capture, retrieval is impossible.

---

# Collection

## Definition

An optional user-created grouping of Memory Cards.

Collections help users organize related information when desired.

---

## Example

Japan Travel

Apartment Hunting

Workout References

---

## Why It Matters

Collections can accelerate retrieval.

They are never required for retrieval.

---

# Context

## Definition

Information that explains why something mattered.

Context provides meaning beyond the original content itself.

---

## Example

- who shared it
- when it was discovered
- why it was saved
- what problem it was connected to

---

## Why It Matters

Context often survives longer than memory of the original content.

---

# Discovery

## Definition

The moment a user encounters potentially valuable information.

Discovery usually occurs outside Mimo.

---

## Example

Finding:

- an article
- a product
- a recommendation
- a video
- a location

on another platform.

---

## Why It Matters

Discovery creates the opportunity for future value.

---

# Discovery Environment

## Definition

Any external platform where information originates before capture.

---

## Examples

- Instagram
- Pinterest
- Reddit
- YouTube
- WhatsApp
- Browser
- Marketplace
- Blog

---

## Why It Matters

Mimo is source agnostic.

The system should support information regardless of origin.

---

# Emergent Organization

## Definition

Organization that develops naturally from accumulated meaning and relationships.

Rather than being imposed before value exists.

---

## Example

Several saved items eventually reveal a common travel theme.

A collection emerges after repeated use.

---

## Why It Matters

Supports retrieval without creating maintenance burden.

---

# Future Value

## Definition

Potential usefulness that may only become relevant later.

---

## Example

A hotel recommendation saved months before planning a trip.

---

## Why It Matters

Future value is the primary reason people save information.

---

# Human Context

## Definition

Meaning explicitly provided by the user.

Represents the user's understanding and intent.

---

## Example

Rahul recommended this.

Compare with the blue version.

Try next leg day.

---

## Why It Matters

The user knows why something mattered.

The system can only infer.

---

# Information Gravity

## Definition

The tendency of information to become valuable when life circumstances create relevance.

---

## Example

A saved restaurant becomes useful when visiting that city.

A saved workout becomes useful when starting a fitness program.

---

## Why It Matters

Information is not always valuable immediately.

Value often emerges later.

---

# Memory Card

## Definition

The primary object within Mimo.

Every captured discovery becomes a Memory Card.

---

## Contains

May include:

- title
- URL
- source
- thumbnail
- context
- relationships
- understanding
- actions

---

## Why It Matters

Everything in Mimo revolves around Memory Cards.

---

# Memory Layer

## Definition

The conceptual role of Mimo.

A system that helps users reconnect with information when it becomes useful.

---

## Why It Matters

Mimo is not merely a storage system.

Its purpose is retrieval and future usefulness.

---

# Personal Context

## Definition

User-provided notes, observations or reminders attached to a Memory Card.

---

## Example

Potential wedding outfit.

Recommended by Rahul.

Use for next vacation.

---

## Why It Matters

Preserves meaning that may not be inferable by the system.

---

# Recognition

## Definition

Identifying desired information through cues rather than exact recall.

---

## Example

Recognizing a thumbnail immediately after seeing it.

---

## Why It Matters

Humans recognize information more effectively than they recall it.

---

# Rediscovery

## Definition

Re-encountering previously forgotten information when it becomes relevant again.

---

## Example

Previously saved Japan travel content resurfacing while planning a trip.

---

## Why It Matters

Rediscovery reconnects users with future value.

---

# Related Memory

## Definition

A Memory Card surfaced because of a meaningful relationship with another Memory Card.

---

## Example

Kyoto Hotel appearing alongside Japan Rail Pass.

---

## Why It Matters

Creates additional retrieval paths.

---

# Related Memories

## Definition

A collection of Memory Cards surfaced because of shared relationships or context.

---

## Why It Matters

Supports rediscovery and retrieval confidence.

---

# Relationship

## Definition

A meaningful connection between Memory Cards.

---

## Examples

Shared:

- topic
- location
- activity
- creator
- recommendation source
- user intent

---

## Why It Matters

Relationships improve findability.

---

# Relationship Network

## Definition

The interconnected graph of relationships between Memory Cards.

---

## Why It Matters

Supports associative retrieval.

Creates alternative retrieval paths.

---

# Retrieval

## Definition

The process of reconnecting a user with previously saved information.

---

## Example

Searching for:

green dress

and finding the saved item.

---

## Why It Matters

Retrieval is the primary source of value in Mimo.

---

# Retrieval Confidence

## Definition

A user's belief that saved information can be recovered successfully.

---

## Why It Matters

Trust grows through successful retrieval.

---

# Retrieval Path

## Definition

Any route that helps a user find desired information.

---

## Examples

- search
- recent activity
- related memories
- collections
- relationships

---

## Why It Matters

Multiple retrieval paths increase findability.

---

# Retrieval Workspace

## Definition

The Home screen experience.

The primary environment for finding and revisiting information.

---

## Contains

- search
- recent activity
- related memories

---

## Why It Matters

Represents the center of the retrieval experience.

---

# Save Confirmation Sheet

## Definition

The feedback surface shown after successful capture.

---

## Contains

- confirmation state
- preview
- view action
- undo action

---

## Why It Matters

Provides confidence that information was preserved successfully.

---

# System Context

## Definition

Meaning inferred by Mimo through AI or system understanding.

Represents an interpretation rather than a known fact.

---

## Example

Possible Future Purchase

Travel Reference

Workout To Try

---

## Why It Matters

Supports retrieval and understanding while remaining distinct from user intent.

---

# Understanding

## Definition

An explanation of why a Memory Card may matter.

May originate from the user or the system.

---

## Examples

Travel Reference

Future Purchase

Recommended by Rahul

---

## Why It Matters

Helps restore meaning during retrieval.

---

# Recent Activity

## Definition

Recently interacted Memory Cards.

---

## Examples

Recently:

- saved
- viewed
- shared
- revisited

---

## Why It Matters

Provides rapid re-entry into previously useful information.

---

# Trust

## Definition

Confidence that Mimo will successfully reconnect users with saved information when needed.

---

## Why It Matters

Trust is the primary long-term outcome of successful retrieval.

---

# Closing Principle

Shared language creates shared understanding.

This glossary exists to ensure that contributors, designers, developers and AI systems describe Mimo consistently and reason about the product using the same vocabulary.
