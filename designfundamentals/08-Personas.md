# Purpose

This document defines the primary behavioral personas supported by Mimo.

These personas are intentionally behavior-driven rather than demographic-driven.

They describe recurring information behaviors that may exist across:

- ages
- professions
- countries
- life stages
- technical abilities

The goal is to understand how people interact with information rather than who they are.

Personas should be used to:

- evaluate product decisions
- validate UX flows
- prioritize features
- identify unmet needs
- guide future research

---

# Persona Framework

Each persona contains four perspectives:

---

## Description

Who this behavioral archetype represents.

---

## Attitudinal Reality

What the user believes, wants or tells themselves.

---

## Behavioral Reality

What the user actually does.

---

## Situational Reality

Environmental factors that influence behavior.

---

These realities frequently differ.

Understanding those differences helps prevent designing for idealized behavior.

---

# Persona 01 — The Collector

## Description

The Collector saves information frequently because many discoveries feel potentially useful.

They rarely know exactly when information will become valuable.

They prefer preserving possibilities rather than risking loss.

---

## Attitudinal Reality

I should probably organize all of this later.

This might be useful someday.

I do not want to lose this.

---

## Behavioral Reality

- saves frequently
- organizes rarely
- accumulates large volumes of content
- forgets much of what was saved
- struggles with retrieval

---

## Situational Reality

Often saving while:

- browsing casually
- scrolling social platforms
- researching unrelated topics
- multitasking

---

## Primary JTBD

Preserve Something Valuable Before It Is Lost.

---

## Primary Pain Points

- information graveyards
- forgotten saves
- retrieval frustration
- organizational guilt

---

## Success Definition

I can save freely without worrying about future organization.

---

# Persona 02 — The Future Planner

## Description

The Future Planner saves information for goals, projects and life situations that may occur later.

The value is expected to emerge over time.

---

## Attitudinal Reality

I will need this eventually.

I am preparing ahead of time.

Future me will thank me.

---

## Behavioral Reality

- saves information months before use
- revisits information after long periods
- often forgets where information was stored
- values preparedness

---

## Situational Reality

Common examples:

- travel planning
- apartment hunting
- wedding planning
- fitness goals
- learning goals
- purchasing decisions

---

## Primary JTBD

Retrieve Information Across Time.

---

## Primary Pain Points

- losing track of plans
- fragmented research
- forgotten discoveries

---

## Success Definition

When the need becomes real, the information is easy to find.

---

# Persona 03 — The Recommendation Keeper

## Description

The Recommendation Keeper places strong value on information shared by trusted people.

The source matters as much as the content itself.

---

## Attitudinal Reality

If someone I trust recommended it, I want to remember it.

The recommendation is part of the value.

---

## Behavioral Reality

- saves recommendations frequently
- remembers people more than titles
- often searches using social context

Examples:

- thing Rahul shared
- restaurant Priya recommended

---

## Situational Reality

Recommendations arrive through:

- conversations
- messaging platforms
- social media
- group chats
- work discussions

---

## Primary JTBD

Preserve Recommendations From People I Trust.

---

## Primary Pain Points

- forgetting recommendation sources
- losing trusted suggestions
- inability to reconnect advice with content

---

## Success Definition

I can remember both the information and who recommended it.

---

# Persona 04 — The Research Explorer

## Description

The Research Explorer accumulates information around a specific topic, goal or decision.

Value emerges from relationships between discoveries.

---

## Attitudinal Reality

I am trying to understand the bigger picture.

Everything connects somehow.

---

## Behavioral Reality

- saves related information rapidly
- jumps between sources
- compares information mentally
- follows curiosity trails

---

## Situational Reality

Examples:

- trip research
- learning a new skill
- evaluating products
- exploring hobbies
- solving a complex problem

---

## Primary JTBD

Reconnect Related Information.

---

## Primary Pain Points

- fragmented knowledge
- disconnected discoveries
- losing context between findings

---

## Success Definition

I can quickly reconstruct the topic I was exploring.

---

# Persona 05 — The Memory Retriever

## Description

The Memory Retriever frequently knows something was saved but struggles to locate it.

Retrieval is the primary challenge.

---

## Attitudinal Reality

I know I saved this somewhere.

I just cannot remember where.

---

## Behavioral Reality

Searches using fragments.

Examples:

- green dress
- AI article
- hotel in Kyoto
- workout reel

Rarely remembers:

- exact title
- exact URL
- exact storage location

---

## Situational Reality

Often attempting retrieval:

- under time pressure
- while multitasking
- after long time gaps

---

## Primary JTBD

Recover Something I Only Partially Remember.

---

## Primary Pain Points

- retrieval failure
- vague memory
- information overload

---

## Success Definition

Partial memory is enough to find the right information.

---

# Persona 06 — The Opportunistic Saver

## Description

The Opportunistic Saver captures information in the middle of other activities.

Speed matters more than organization.

---

## Attitudinal Reality

I will deal with this later.

I just do not want to lose it.

---

## Behavioral Reality

- captures rapidly
- delays organization
- often forgets immediately after saving
- values convenience

---

## Situational Reality

Examples:

- commuting
- standing in line
- between meetings
- during conversations
- late-night browsing

---

## Primary JTBD

Preserve Something Valuable Before It Is Lost.

---

## Primary Pain Points

- capture friction
- interruptions
- context switching

---

## Success Definition

Saving takes almost no effort.

# Shared Human Realities

Across all personas, several realities remain consistent.

---

## Imperfect Memory

People forget.

The system should expect forgetting.

---

## Fragmented Recall

Users remember fragments rather than complete information.

---

## Limited Attention

Interactions occur during busy moments.

Attention is often divided.

---

## Future-Oriented Saving

Information is frequently preserved for future usefulness.

---

## Low-Maintenance Preference

Users prefer systems that do not require continuous management.

---

## Desire For Confidence

Users want confidence that saved information remains recoverable.

# Design Implications

The product should support:

- imperfect memory
- contextual retrieval
- low-friction capture
- relationship-based navigation
- future-oriented information use

The product should avoid assuming:

- perfect organization
- perfect recall
- continuous maintenance
- uninterrupted attention

---

# Closing Principle

Mimo is designed around information behavior rather than identity.

The goal is not to serve a particular demographic.

The goal is to support the recurring ways humans discover, save, forget, remember and reuse information.
