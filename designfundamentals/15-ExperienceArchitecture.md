# Purpose

This document defines the experience architecture of Mimo.

While Information Architecture defines what exists and UX Flows define what happens, Experience Architecture defines how the product should feel as users move through it.

This document establishes:

- experience goals
- emotional outcomes
- cognitive load principles
- information pacing
- interaction rhythm
- retrieval experience principles
- AI interaction principles

Its purpose is to guide future design decisions and ensure consistency across all user experiences.

---

# Experience Vision

Mimo should feel like a trusted memory companion.

Users should feel confident that valuable information remains accessible even when details have been forgotten.

The experience should reduce mental effort rather than create additional work.

The system should feel calm, reliable and supportive.

Never demanding.

Never distracting.

Never overwhelming.

---

# Desired User Outcomes

Users should feel:

- confident
- capable
- oriented
- supported
- reassured
- in control

Users should not feel:

- overwhelmed
- pressured
- manipulated
- confused
- rushed
- dependent on the system

---

# Core Experience Principles

---

## Confidence Through Retrieval

The most important emotional outcome is retrieval confidence.

Users should gradually develop the belief:

I can find this later.

Every successful retrieval strengthens this belief.

Every retrieval failure weakens it.

---

## Recognition Before Effort

Users should identify information before they are required to think about it.

Visual and contextual cues should appear before deeper interpretation.

The system should minimize cognitive effort whenever possible.

---

## Meaning Before Action

Users should understand why information matters before being asked what they want to do with it.

Actions should follow understanding.

Not precede it.

---

## Calm Assistance

The product should assist quietly.

Support should appear when useful.

The system should never compete for attention.

---

## Progressive Depth

Complexity should appear gradually.

Users should encounter the simplest useful experience first.

Additional depth should emerge naturally when needed.

---

# Experience Rhythm

Most software alternates between decisions and actions.

Mimo follows a different rhythm.

Recognition

↓

Understanding

↓

Action

The product should help users reconstruct meaning before asking them to make decisions.

This rhythm should be visible throughout the application.

---

# Experience Layers

The experience unfolds in layers.

Recognition

↓

Context

↓

Understanding

↓

Relationships

↓

Actions

This mirrors the architecture of Memory Cards and retrieval experiences.

---

---

## Understanding

---

## Relationships

## Actions

# Cognitive Load Principles

The experience should continuously reduce mental effort.

---

## Minimize Decisions

Avoid unnecessary choices.

Every decision creates friction.

---

## Reduce Memory Burden

The system should remember information so users do not have to.

---

## Avoid Administrative Work

Users should not spend significant effort managing structures.

---

## Prioritize Recognition

Recognition requires less effort than recall.

Visual and contextual cues should support retrieval.

---

## Preserve Orientation

Users should always understand:

- where they are
- what they are viewing
- why it appeared

# Retrieval Experience Principles

Every retrieval experience should answer:

Is this it?

before:

What can I do with it?

Recognition precedes action.

Meaning precedes action.

Understanding precedes action.

This ordering should remain consistent across the application.

---

# Context Reconstruction Principles

Time erodes meaning.

The product should help users rebuild meaning quickly.

Whenever possible, retrieval should restore:

- original intent
- recommendation source
- discovery circumstances
- related information

The objective is not merely finding information.

The objective is understanding why it mattered.

---

# Relationship Experience Principles

Relationships should feel helpful rather than clever.

Users should understand:

Why are these related?

before:

What are these?

Relationship explanations should remain visible and understandable.

---

# AI Experience Principles

AI should feel:

- visible
- understandable
- supportive
- optional

AI should never feel:

- magical
- controlling
- invasive
- unpredictable

Users should always understand:

- what they created
- what the system inferred
- why something appeared

Transparency creates trust.

---

# Capture Experience Principles

Saving should feel effortless.

Users should never feel pressure to:

- organize immediately
- categorize immediately
- clean up immediately

Capture should feel lightweight.

Meaning can emerge later.

---

# Rediscovery Experience Principles

Rediscovery should feel relevant.

Not random.

Not promotional.

Not engagement-driven.

The ideal reaction is:

I forgot I saved this.

This is useful right now.

---

Not:

Why is this being shown to me?

# Trust Architecture

Trust develops gradually.

Successful Retrieval

↓

Confidence

↓

Future Saving

↓

Richer Archive

↓

Better Retrieval

↓

Deeper Trust

The experience should continuously strengthen this loop.

---

# Anti-Patterns

The experience intentionally rejects:

---

## Feed Behavior

Information should not become content consumption.

---

## Attention Competition

The product should not compete for user attention.

---

## Gamification

No streaks.

No rewards.

No artificial incentives.

---

## Artificial Urgency

No pressure-based interaction patterns.

---

## Organizational Burden

No mandatory maintenance workflows.

---

## AI Mysticism

No unexplained intelligence.

No hidden reasoning.

---

# Success Criteria

A successful experience allows users to:

- save without friction
- retrieve without perfect memory
- reconstruct meaning quickly
- act confidently
- trust future retrieval

without feeling overwhelmed or burdened.

---

# Closing Principle

The experience succeeds when users trust their future ability to reconnect with information without needing to remember how they stored it.

The product should feel like an extension of memory rather than a system that requires management.
