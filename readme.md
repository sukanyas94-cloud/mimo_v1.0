# Mimo

Version: 1.0
Status: Frozen Candidate

---

# Overview

Mimo is a mobile-first memory layer for the internet.

People discover valuable information every day across:

- Instagram
- Pinterest
- Reddit
- WhatsApp
- YouTube
- Browsers
- Marketplaces
- Blogs and articles

They save those discoveries with the intention of revisiting them later.

Unfortunately, most saved content eventually becomes difficult to find, difficult to understand, or completely forgotten.

Mimo exists to solve this problem.

Instead of treating saved links as bookmarks, Mimo treats them as memories.

The goal is not simply to preserve URLs.

The goal is to preserve future value.

---

# Problem Statement

People are excellent at discovering useful information.

People are terrible at retrieving it later.

Current solutions often fail because they rely on:

- folders
- categories
- tags
- perfect recall
- manual organization

Over time these systems become difficult to maintain.

As saved content grows, retrieval becomes harder.

Users frequently remember:

- an image
- a person
- a topic
- a situation
- a recommendation

but not the exact title, location or storage path.

Mimo is designed around how human memory actually works.

---

# Product Vision

Create the most trusted personal memory layer for internet discoveries.

Users should feel confident that information worth saving today will remain retrievable tomorrow, regardless of:

- platform
- source
- time passed
- organizational effort

---

# Core Product Promise

> If I save it, I can find it later.

Every product decision should strengthen this promise.

---

# What Mimo Is

Mimo is:

- a personal memory layer
- a retrieval-first product
- a preservation tool
- a context-aware archive
- a relationship-driven memory system

Mimo helps users:

- save discoveries
- retrieve information
- understand why it mattered
- rediscover related content
- act on previously saved information

---

# What Mimo Is Not

Mimo is not:

- a social network
- a content feed
- a bookmarking utility
- a note-taking application
- a project management tool
- a productivity platform
- a personal knowledge management system

Users should never feel responsible for maintaining Mimo.

Organization should remain optional.

Retrieval should remain primary.

---

# Core Behaviors

Mimo is designed around five primary user behaviors.

## Save

Preserve something valuable before it is lost.

Examples:

- article
- video
- product
- place
- recommendation

---

## Retrieve

Find previously saved information using partial memory.

Examples:

- "green dress"
- "thing Rahul shared"
- "Kyoto hotel"

---

## Understand

Recover context and meaning.

Examples:

- why it was saved
- who shared it
- when it mattered

---

## Rediscover

Reconnect related memories when relevant.

Examples:

- Japan trip planning
- marathon training
- apartment hunting

---

## Act

Use information after retrieval.

Examples:

- open original
- share
- purchase
- revisit
- learn

---

# Product Principles

The repository is built around several foundational principles.

## Retrieval First

Retrieval generates value.

Saving creates potential value.

---

## Recognition Before Recall

Users recognize more effectively than they remember.

Visual recognition should be prioritized.

---

## Save First

Capture should never be blocked by organization.

---

## Context Matters

Meaning is often more important than metadata.

---

## Relationships Matter

People remember through association.

---

## Organization Is Optional

The product should not require maintenance.

---

## AI Assists

AI strengthens retrieval.

AI does not replace user intent.

---

## Trust Over Engagement

The goal is confidence.

Not screen time.

Not retention mechanics.

Not attention optimization.

---

# Product Architecture

The product revolves around a single core object.

## Memory Card

Every saved discovery becomes a Memory Card.

Memory Cards preserve:

- access
- context
- understanding
- relationships
- future usefulness

Everything else in the product exists to support Memory Cards.
